# Scrap Finance Primer

## Industry Context
The UK scrap metal and recycling industry is characterized by high turnover, volatile margins, and complex operational logistics. The gap between physical yard activity (weighbridges, transport, grading) and financial reporting (Xero, Sage) is often wide, leading to decision-making in the dark.

## Core Pain Points

1.  **Stock Discrepancies:** The physical stock on the ground rarely matches the system stock. Upgrades, downgrades, moisture loss, and dirt all affect yield. Without rigorous stock sheets, month-end profit is a guess.
2.  **Transport Costs:** Moving metal is expensive. If transport costs aren't accurately allocated to specific deals or material categories, gross margins are falsely inflated.
3.  **System Disconnection:** Many yards use specialized software like Fred for weighbridge and operations, but struggle to map that data cleanly into their accounting software (e.g., Xero). This results in manual re-keying and coding errors.
4.  **EWC Compliance:** European Waste Catalogue (EWC) codes must be accurate for environmental compliance, but they also need to map logically to financial nominal codes.

## The Value Proposition
An outsourced Finance Director in this sector doesn't just look at the P&L. They must understand the yard. The value comes from:
- Reconciling Fred data to Xero.
- Building accurate KPI dashboards (tonnes processed, margin per tonne, transport cost per tonne).
- Creating reliable stock visibility processes.
- Automating manual deal sheets.
